Ongoing Long-Term Projects: Japanese Aesthetics

I explore the integration and continuity between life, nature and beyond. I do hope my photography can evoke that connection and express its beauty, make the ordinary special.
As a simple everyday moment can be surprising and unpredictable, my images are authentic shots in a documentary style where I don't influence the scene.

Selected Images Inspired by the Japanese Philosophy

Kacho Fugetsu (花鳥風月かち ょう ふう げつ) ["ka-chi yo-u fu-u ge-tsu"] —The Beauty of Nature

The term consists of the kanji (characters) for 'flower, bird, wind, moon,' and is collectively referred to as the "beauties of nature." This philosophy is considered one of the most important concepts in Japanese aesthetics about natural beauty and art.

Wabi-Sabi (侘寂わび さび) —Appreciation of imperfection, impermanence, and incompleteness

Refers to a Japanese philosophy that values rustic simplicity, transience and imperfection of life. The aesthetic is sometimes described as one of beauty that is imperfect, impermanent, and incomplete.
